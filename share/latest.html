Introduction to Snaps

June 19, 2019

Snaps are universal Linux packages. Basically, they make it easy to distribute applications amongst various Linux distributions, so I can use the same command (and specifically the same package name) to install applications I need on my Arch machine as well as my KDE Neon machine. There are other added benefits (and some minor drawbacks) of snaps such as improved security from confinement and ultimate re-usability and convenience from using snapshots.

I recently tried to snap a few applications of my own. I have had some ("limited" is probably a more fitting term) with using Arch's build system, so figured I'd give it a shot. I watched a YouTube video by one of my favorite members in the open source community, Mr. Alan Pope. I was able to follow along and successfully build the application he used in his example, but when it came to packaging my own (mine meaning others' applications), I was unsuccessful.

I tried packaging two command-line applications, pianobar and beets. The former is a console-based client for Pandora and the latter is a colsole-based music metadata modifier/library manager. There are a few other beginner tutorials available on the Snapcraft.io blog as well as a an open forum. Hopefully I can given them a read when I find the time and finsh those two snaps.

On Deleting Instagram

June 8, 2019

One of the main objectives of creating this website, aside from my general interest in web design and the ability to record parts of my life, was to rid myself of social media accounts. The more I used social media, the more I felt the social aspect had less to do with socializing and more with boasting or asserting one's societal status. See also, The Agony of Instagram. The older I've gotten, the less important the politics of social media (or politics in general really) has become to me.

For the last year or so, I've almost completely detached myself from the online social ecosystem (and honestly it's been great). In addition, being somewhat of a luddite with large Into the Wild influences, I often ask myself what there is to gain from it. On numerous occassions, I've deleted entire accounts which were active for years. Afterwards, I noticed my life hadn't even skipped a beat. That being said, I have come to the conclusion there are somethings were documenting which I intend to do so here.

More than anything, this site is for those that remain interested in my life. I can't say I understand why anyone would be, but it is nevertheless appreciated. I will do my best in order to meet those requests. Thanks again to all of those that are interested. I know right now, this method is slightly out-of-the-way, but I do have plans of making it convenient for those that do feel inclined.

Also, I simply hate Instagram's ridiculous terms of service. If you weren't already aware, Instagram photos aren’t really yours: Someone else can sell them for $90,000.
