September 2019

September 16, 2019

When I first made this blog, I think part of me wanted a place to whine and moan. It's not often I tell people my problems. I'm sure there is benefit from working through problems with others, but I've generally found it most useful to them handle my way. Of course, this is not always trust. Nevertheless, I haven't really felt the urge to convey my issues with others, and I think it had been for the best.

It's been a while since I wrote the previous post. I don't remember much of it nor do I intend to read it anytime soon. I do remember the general sentiment though and I think anyone that knew me years ago would have questioned if the same person they knew wrote it.

I'm not sure when it started, but I started noticing two things changing about myself. For most people, the changes would be simply a byproduce of developing into an adult. For me, I consider them regressions in behavior.

First, I noticed myself becoming less competitive. I think this is pretty common as one ages. It wasn't an issue for me in college, as I became less focused about getting the highest grade and more focused on getting the best grade I could. However, past college, it kept trending downwards until I felt myself becoming content with most things. I then realized, with the decrease in competitiveness came a decrease in passion. Things no longer mattered to me because I was no longer getting invested in them.

Second, I noticed myself doing what other wanted or thought I should be doing more. I never really cared if what I did pleased others. Additionally, I would not consider myself on the bell-curve in normality. For example, I found myself stressing about not having brought a single partner to a family gathering. For me, it is more important to bring the partner to the gathering. That is to say, I only think it is appropriate to invite a partner you're completely interested in rather than inviting someone you're half interested in to please others' expectations.

Now that I'm no longer embarassed by the most recent post, I'm thinking I might post more again. We'll see. I might even set aside a place for more formal discussions. I want to keep this mostly off the cuff with little hesitation, but think there is utility in going in depth and formalizing strong opinions on topics.

All said and done, I'm back to doing what I want and think is best and not really giving a fuck if others think I should be doing differently.

Introduction to Snaps

June 19, 2019

Snaps are universal Linux packages. Basically, they make it easy to distribute applications amongst various Linux distributions, so I can use the same command (and specifically the same package name) to install applications I need on my Arch machine as well as my KDE Neon machine. There are other added benefits (and some minor drawbacks) of snaps such as improved security from confinement and ultimate re-usability and convenience from using snapshots.

I recently tried to snap a few applications of my own. I have had some ("limited" is probably a more fitting term) with using Arch's build system, so figured I'd give it a shot. I watched a YouTube video by one of my favorite members in the open source community, Mr. Alan Pope. I was able to follow along and successfully build the application he used in his example, but when it came to packaging my own (mine meaning others' applications), I was unsuccessful.

I tried packaging two command-line applications, pianobar and beets. The former being a console-based client for Pandora and the latter a console-based music metadata modifier/library manager. There are a few other beginner tutorials available on the Snapcraft.io blog as well as an open forum. Hopefully I can given them a read when I find the time and finsh those two snaps.

On Deleting Instagram

June 8, 2019

One of the main objectives of creating this website, aside from my general interest in web design and the ability to record parts of my life, was to rid myself of social media accounts. The more I used social media, the more I felt the social aspect had less to do with socializing and more with boasting or asserting one's societal status. See also, The Agony of Instagram. The older I've gotten, the less important the politics of social media (or politics in general really) has become to me.

For the last year or so, I've almost completely detached myself from the online social ecosystem (and honestly it's been great). In addition, being somewhat of a luddite with large Into the Wild influences, I often ask myself what there is to gain from it. On numerous occassions, I've deleted entire accounts which were active for years. Afterwards, I noticed my life hadn't even skipped a beat. That being said, I have come to the conclusion there are somethings were documenting which I intend to do so here.

More than anything, this site is for those that remain interested in my life. I can't say I understand why anyone would be, but it is nevertheless appreciated. I will do my best in order to meet those requests. Thanks again to all of those that are interested. I know right now, this method is slightly out-of-the-way, but I do have plans of making it convenient for those that do feel inclined.

Also, I simply hate Instagram's ridiculous terms of service. If you weren't already aware, Instagram photos aren’t really yours: Someone else can sell them for $90,000.
